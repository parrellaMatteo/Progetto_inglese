Video Codecs: The Compression Arms Race

A codec (coder-decoder) is an algorithm that compresses raw video data. Each generation is more efficient — meaning better quality at smaller file sizes.

H.264
Released 2003
The internet standard. Used by 80%+ of all streaming. Well-supported on every device.
H.265
Released 2013
Also called HEVC. 50% more efficient than H.264. Used for 4K streaming on Netflix and Disney+.
VP9
Released 2013
Google's open-source alternative to H.265. Used by YouTube for HD and 4K content.
AV1
Released 2018
The future standard. 30% more efficient than H.265, completely royalty-free. Netflix is migrating to it.

CDN: The Secret Backbone

A Content Delivery Network is a system of servers distributed around the world. Instead of every request going to Netflix's headquarters in California, the video data is cached on a server near you — perhaps in Milan or Frankfurt. This reduces latency and prevents overload.

Netflix CDN Nodes~17,000+ worldwide
Data served per day>1 Exabyte (1,000 TB)
Average distance to user<100km
Cache hit rate~95%

Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR)

Netflix doesn't send you a single quality video. It prepares every film in 20+ quality levels — from 240p (300 Kbps) to 4K HDR (25 Mbps). Every few seconds, your device measures your internet speed and requests the appropriate chunk.

# Example: HLS Playlist (what your device receives) #EXTM3U #EXT-X-STREAM-INF:BANDWIDTH=300000,RESOLUTION=426x240 movie_240p.m3u8 #EXT-X-STREAM-INF:BANDWIDTH=1500000,RESOLUTION=1280x720 movie_720p.m3u8 #EXT-X-STREAM-INF:BANDWIDTH=8000000,RESOLUTION=1920x1080 movie_1080p.m3u8 #EXT-X-STREAM-INF:BANDWIDTH=25000000,RESOLUTION=3840x2160 movie_4K_HDR.m3u8

DRM: How Piracy is (Mostly) Prevented

Digital Rights Management is the technology that prevents you from downloading and sharing Netflix content. The video is encrypted with a key that is only temporarily granted to your device's "trusted execution environment" — a secure chip inside your phone or laptop. The movie never exists in an unencrypted state on your device.
